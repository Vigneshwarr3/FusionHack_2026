§ 01 — The pitch

End goal in one sentence

A shareable, deck.gl dashboard that a journalist, policy analyst, or county extension agent can open and answer, per county, five questions: how much aquifer is left, when it runs dry, which crops drained it, what policy levers would change the trajectory, and — crucially — what source backs every number on screen.

The demo anchor (spec §8 Day 3) is a time scrubber from 1950 to 2100 — drag it, watch 606 counties desaturate as the Ogallala empties. That is the "oh sh*t" visual. Everything else hangs off it.

The problem isn't that the Ogallala is draining. It's that no one can show a county board, in one view, how fast and who's responsible.
§ 02 — Targets

What we're actually predicting

Three annual, per-county targets. Attribution on top. Uncertainty where we can defend it.

§ 03 — Data

How we use what we ingested

Three data regimes per county. The model has to handle all three without pretending the tiers are equivalent.

Tier Counties Source Role
modeled_high 248 / 606 WIZARD · NGWMN · TWDB · NE DEE Training labels + ground truth for validation
modeled_low ~358 Bedrock × 0.5 estimate · HPA-median fallback Targets for GBDT imputation (next sprint)
metered pending KS Open Records Request (filed 2026-04-18) Validation labels + TX imputation training data

Features already joined in the baseline parquet

  • Crop mix × IWMS pumping rates → inferred annual pumping per county
  • Bedrock depth, aquifer footprint fraction (KGS + USGS geology)
  • Neighboring-county thickness for spatial autocorrelation signal
  • When PRISM lands: annual precipitation anomaly
  • When KS ORR lands: metered pumpage as a second-opinion label
⚠ The 0.52× calibration

Our inferred pumping is roughly half of USGS 2015 reported. Documented in docs/limitations.md §4b. Percentage deltas are unaffected; absolute CO₂-Mt numbers are ~2× understated. We frame this as a conservative estimate in the UI — the honesty is the credibility feature, not a bug to hide.

§ 04 — Modeling

A tiered approach, shipped end-to-end

Ship the thinnest version of every tier before improving any one of them. That's how the demo stays alive while each layer gets better.

Tier 1 · Physics baseline
done

Deterministic volumetric balance:

thickness(t+1) = thickness(t) + (recharge − pumping) / (area × specific_yield)

The scenario engine mutates pumping or recharge, re-runs forward from 1950. Already in compose_baseline.py + scenario engine. All 34 tests green.

Tier 2 · GBDT imputation
Vignesh's lane

Train on 248 modeled_high counties: features → (thickness, decline).

Predict for the 358 modeled_low counties to replace the crude bedrock/median fallback.

Validate via leave-one-state-out CV: hold out KS, predict from NE+CO+TX+…, report RMSE.

Expected win: fallback RMSE ~15 m → GBDT RMSE ~5–7 m (Deines 2019 benchmark).

Tier 3 · Scenario deltas
done

Six counterfactuals mutate inputs then re-run the Tier 1 forward sim:

  1. Baseline — business-as-usual
  2. KS LEMA — 20% pumping cut
  3. Corn → sorghum crop swap
  4. 2012-level drought recurrence
  5. No-till / cover-crop recharge boost
  6. Custom slider — user-defined pumping Δ
Tier 4 · Attribution
done

Per-crop share of county pumping:

crop_share = (acres × iwms_rate) / Σ(acres × iwms_rate)

Pure arithmetic. No ML. Drives the "who's responsible" view.

Headroom — only if time permits

  • Temporal fusion transformer on well-level time series
  • Vignesh's ag foundation model embeddings as extra features
  • Quantile regression → confidence bands on the time scrubber
§ 05 — Stakes

Why the prediction matters

The Ogallala supports ~$35B / yr of agricultural output across 8 states. Recharge (0.5–3 in/yr) runs roughly 10× slower than withdrawal — depletion is effectively irreversible on human timescales.

Policy is a state-by-state patchwork: Kansas LEMA districts, Texas rule of capture, Nebraska NRD governance. No one has a comparable county-level accountability view — journalists and county boards rely on state-aggregate USGS reports that average away the counties that are already catastrophically dry.

Our contribution

A defensible, county-granular, methodology-transparent number someone can cite in a city council meeting or a Guardian article. The 0.52× USGS footnote in docs/limitations.md isn't embarrassment — it's the credibility stamp.

§ 06 — Frontend

What the UI should strive for

Built as a new tab in the parent Next.js app — not a separate app. Reuses deck.gl + Cobbles & Currents design tokens from web_app/src/utils/design.ts. The aesthetic anchor: Bloomberg Terminal meets NYT Upshot, not a gov.gov dashboard.

Must-have — demo-critical

Deck.gl choropleth
606 HPA counties, color = saturated thickness remaining. Pan, zoom, hover for county stats.
Time scrubber 1950–2100
Drag it. Watch counties desaturate and flip red. This is the demo-anchor visual.
Scenario panel
Six buttons on the left rail. Instant re-run (<200ms) on cached baselines.
County drill-down
Click a county → panel with thickness, years-to-depletion, per-crop pumping stack, data-quality badge.
Methodology page
Every number on screen links to its source + its limitation note. Transparency is the whole product.
